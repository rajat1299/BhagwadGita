I meditate on two-armed Krsna, who is dark like

a monsoon rain cloud, dressed in yellow garments,

garlanded with forest flowers, crowned with a pea-

cock feather and decorated with lotuses. His face

is as splendid as millions of moons and His eyes

move restlessly. His forehead is marked with tilaka

made of sandalwood paste and musk. He is adorned

with eattings that resemble two rising suns, and His

perspitation-anointed cheeks are like two glistening

mitrots. His eyes with raised eyebrows above them

playfully glance at His beloved’s face. The tip of His

graceful raised nose is decorated with a glistening

pearl. His lips are red like the bimba fruit and are

splendid in the moonlight of His teeth. His hands

are splendid with bracelets, armlets, and jewelled

rings. He holds a flute in His left lotus hand, His

waist is splendid with a ९५१८८ ful belt and His feet are

splendid with beautiful anklets. His eyes are rest-

less with the nectar of His divine activities and He

jokes with His friends, making them laugh again and

again. He sits on a jewelled throne under a desire

tree in the forest of Wrndavana with His beloved.

In this way one should meditate on Sti Krsna. (San-

at-kumara Sambita 54-62)
