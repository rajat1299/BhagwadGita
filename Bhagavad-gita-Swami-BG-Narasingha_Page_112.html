Sti Kysna states in the above verses that He has no duty

to petform, that He lacks nothing not has He anything to

gain. In other words, Krsna is already perfect and complete

— ort piirnam. Yet Krsna acts; He 15 not idle. He acts for

the benefit of humanity by establishing spititual practices

in every age —dharmar tu saksad bhagavat-pranitarn. When

an avatara of the Absolute Truth makes His appearance in

the material world, He does so to establish eternal princi-

ples of dharma.

Krsna appeared at the end of Dvapara-yuga, 5,237

yeats ago (3228 BCE) and spoke the Bhagavad-gita. How-

ever, this was not Kysna’s most recent appearance. Krsna

appeared again after the beginning of Kali-yuga 525

yeats ago. This Kali-yuga avatara of Krsna is known as

Sti Caitanya Mahaprabhu. In His avatara as Sti Caitanya

Mahaprabhu, Krsna taught the process of sankirtana, the

congregational chanting of the maba-mantra, to accompany

the study of Bhagavad-gita. As regards the maha-mantra,

the Kalt-santarana Upanisad states as follows:

hare krsna hare krsna krsna krsna hare bare

bare rama bare rama rama rama bare bare
