Yogis who are free from vice, self-controlled, whose doubts

have been removed and who are engaged in welfare work

for all living beings, attain liberation in the Supreme.

VERSE 26

कामकोधविमुक्तानां यतीनां यतचेतसाम्‌ |

अभितो ब्रह्मनिर्वाणं वर्तते विदितात्मनाम्‌ ॥२६॥

kama-krodha-vimuktanam yatinam yata-cetasam

abbito brabma-nirvanam vartate viditatmanam

Those sannyasis who constantly endeavour for perfection,

who have controlled their minds, who are self-realised and

who are free from lust and anger, soon achieve liberation

in the Supreme.

The yogi who shuts out the external sense-objects and fixes

his concentration between the eyebrows, stabilising the

inward and outward. breaths that flow through the nostrils

and thus controls the senses, the mind and intelligence,

is dedicated to the attainment of liberation. He is never

bound. by desires stemming from lust, fear and anger and is

certainly always liberated.
