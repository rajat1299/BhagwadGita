yat tad avyaktam ajaram acintyam ajam aksayam

anirdesyam artipath ca pani-padady-asarnyu tam

vibburh sarva-gatarh nityarh bhata-yonim akaranam

vyapya-vyapta yatab sarvarh tad vai pasyanti sirayab

The Brahman feature of the Supreme is unmanifest,

unattected by time, inconceivable, without material

origin, free from decay and diminution, indescrib-

able, formless, without hands, feet, or other limbs,

all-powerful, a -pervading, eternal, the origin of all

material elements, without any material cause, pres-

ent in everything, although nothing is situated in it,

the source of the material cosmos and the object of

vision for the demigods. (Visnu Purana 6.5.66-67).

That effulgence of Krsna is non-different from Him

and shines like millions, and millions of sans.

vasudevad abbinnas tu vabny-arkendu-Sata-prabham

vasudevo ‘pi bhagavarns tad-dharma paramesvarab

svatit (11 ksobhayaty eva tejasa tena vai yutam

prakasa-riipo bhagavan acyutam casakrd dvija

The effulgence of the impersonal Brahman is

like unlimited fires, sans and moons. Brahman is

non-different from Vasudeva (Krsna). Vasudeva is

full of all auspicious qualities and His nature is that

He is the Supreme Controller. When He removes

the covering of His own Brahman effulgence, Krsna

reveals His original, eternal, transcendental form.

(Narada-paficaratra)
