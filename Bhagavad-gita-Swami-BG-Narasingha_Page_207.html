The first person to provide evidence and infer the

existence of the phenomenon of Dark Matter was the

Swiss astrophysicist Fritz Zwicky, of the California Insti-

tute of Technology in 1933. Zwicky applied the viral

theorem to the Coma cluster of galaxies and obtained evi-

dence of unseen mass. Zwicky estimated the cluster’s total

mass based on the motions of galaxies neat its edge and

compared that estimate to one based on the number of

galaxies and total brightness of the cluster. He found that

there was about four-hundred times more estimated mass

than was visually observable. The gravity of the visible

galaxies in the cluster would be far too small for such fast

orbits, so something extta was required. This is known as

the “Missing Mass Problem’. Based on these conclusions,

Zwicky inferred that there must be some non-visible form

of matter that would provide enough of the mass and

gtavity to hold the cluster together. That was the begin-

ning of the search for Dark Matter.
