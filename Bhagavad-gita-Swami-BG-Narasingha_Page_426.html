ग BHAGAVAD-GITA oye

anudvega-karamn vakyarh satyarh priyabitarh ca yat

svadhyayabhyasanath caiva vanmayarh tapa ucyate

Truthful speech that does not disturb others, which is

pleasing and beneficial as well as the recitation of the Vedas

— this is known as verbal austerity.

VERSE 16

मनःप्रसादः सौम्यत्वं मौनमात्मविनियहः ।

भावसंशुचिरित्येतत्तपो मानसमुच्यते ॥ ९६॥

manab prasadab 11 atma-vinigrabab

bhava-sarnsuddbir ity-ctat tapo manasam ucyate

Mental austerity is said to be peace of mind, gentleness,

silence, self-control and purity of heart.

VERSE 17

TET परया तप्तं TIAA नरैः ।

अफलाकाङ्खिमिरयक्तैः सात्त्विकं परिचक्षते ॥ ९७॥

Sraddbaya paraya taptarh tapas tat tri-vidbarh narath

apbalakanksibbir yuktath sattvikarh paricaksate

When these three types of austerity are taken up with

strong faith by one who is strict and who is without selfish

motivation, they are said to be in the mode of goodness.
