yatravatirnath krsnakbyarh

pararn brabma narakrtim

When the Supreme Person descends in His human-

like form, He is Krsna, the Supreme Brahman.

(Visau Purana 4.11.2)

tasmat krsna eva paro devas tatit dhyayet

tart raset tarh bhajet tarh yajet

Thus Krsna is the Supreme Person. One should

meditate on Him. One should delight in Him. One

should worship Him and make offerings to Him.

(Gopala-tapani Upanisad 1.54)

krsir bbii-vacakab sabdo nas ca nirvrti-vacakab

tayor aikyarh pararh brabma krsna ityabbidbtyate

The verbal root krs refers to the all-attractive quality

of Krsna, and the syllable wa refers to His spiritual

bliss. When ks is added to the affix na, it becomes

the word Krsna, indicating the Supreme Truth.

(Mahabharata, Udyoga-parva 71.4)
