Arjuna said: O Krsna, seeing all my relatives assembled

here and preparing for battle, the strength drains from my

limbs and my mouth becomes parched.

Sti Krsna is known as Partha-sarathi, the chariot-driver

of Arjuna. Because Krsna was Arjuna’s friend and compan-

ion, he requested Krsna to draw his chariot between the

two armies so that he could see with whom he was to do

battle. But upon seeing the enemy before him, Arjana was

shocked and fell into a state of bewilderment.

Now the stage at Karuksetra was set, so that Sti Krsna

could speak Bhagavad-gita — Arjuna became overwhelmed

with grief and gave up his duty. As a warrior Arjuna was

duty-bound to fight, bat seeing the ordeal that lay ahead

he could not proceed.
