A ksatriya, when called to battle, should never aban-

don his duty under any circumstance. Krsna tells Arjuna

that if he is killed in battle while defending the king-

dom, such an act of heroism will promote him to a higher

status in his next life. Or, Krsna says, if he 1s victorious in

battle then he will regain the kingdom and enjoy life on

Earth. In any case, Krsna strongly encourages Arjuna not

to abandon his duty.

VERSE 39

एषा तेऽभिहिता सांख्ये बुदधिर्योगि त्विमां शरणु ।

वुधा युक्तो यया पाथं क्म॑वन्धं परहास्यसि ॥३९॥

esa te’bbibita sankhye buddbir-yoge tv-imarh Srnu

buddbya yukto yaya partha karma-bandbarh prabasyasi

O Arjuna, son of Prtha, I have revealed to you the knowl-

edge of individual consciousness. Now hear how to act

upon this knowledge, through which you will be able to

release yourself from the bondage of action.

नेहाभिकमनाशोऽस्ति प्रत्यवायो न विद्यते |

nebabbikrama naso’sti pratyavayo na vidyate

svalpam apyasya dbarmasya trayate mabato bhayat

There is no loss, nor is there any diminution of result in

performing this dharma. Even the slightest effort saves one
