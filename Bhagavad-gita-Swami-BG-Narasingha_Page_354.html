VERSE 25

ध्यानेनात्मनि Plea केचिदात्मानमात्मना ।

अन्ये सांख्येन योगेन कर्मयोगेन चापरे ॥२५॥

dhyanenatmani pasyanti kecid atmanam atmana

anye santkbyena yogena karma-~yogena capare

Through meditation, some yogis glimpse the Supreme

within the heart. Others see Him through the process of

analysis (sankhya), while others perceive Him through kar-

ma-yoga.

There are also those that do not know these methods, but

simply by hearing about Him from others, they engage in

His worship. Because they have faith in what they have

heard, they too transcend death.

It is described that both material nature (prakrti) and

the living beings (jivatma ot purusa) are without beginning

(anadi). This means that material nature and the living

beings exist as eternal energies of Sti Krsna before the

process of creation takes place. The living being’s origin

is in the tatastha, or matginal plane, before the onslaught

of time. In this regard, the origin of the living beings and

material nature are both anadi, or having no first cause.
