अ CHAPTER 1 Aye

yadyapyete na pasyanti lobbopabatachetasab

kula~ksayakrtarn dosarit mitra~drobe ca patakam

katharh na jiieyam asmabbib papad asman nivartitum

kula-ksayakrtarh dosarh prapasyadbbir janardana

0 Janardana, although the hearts of these men are over-

whelmed by greed and they cannot see the fault in betraying

one’s friends and the offence of murdering one’s kinsmen,

why should we engage in such a terrible activity, knowing

well the consequences?

VERSE 39

कुलक्षये ed कुरुधर्माः सनातनाः ।

धर्म नष्टे कुलं कृत्त्रमधर्मोऽभिभवत्युत ॥३९॥

kula-ksaye pranasyanti kula-~dharmab sanatanab

dharme naste kularh krisnam adbarmo’bbibbavatyuta

By destroying one’s relatives, the family traditions are van-

quished. forever, and when such practices perish, unright-

eousness prevails over the entire dynasty.

VERSE 40

अधर्माभिभवात्कृष्ण प्रदुष्यन्ति HORA: |

AY FAY वार्ष्णेय जायते AST: ॥४०॥

adharmabbibbavat krsna pradusyanti kula-striyab

strisu dustasu varsneya jayate varna~sattkarab

0 Krsna, descendant of Vrsni, when unrighteousness

prevails then the women of the family become degraded.

When the women become degraded, then undesirable off-

spring is the result.
