sukbam atyantikarn yat tad buddbi-grabyam atindriyam

vetti yatra na caivayarh sthitas calati tattvatab

yath labdbva caparar labbarh manyate nadbikarh tatab

yasmin sthito na dubkhena gurunapi vicalyate

tarn vidyad dubkha~sarnyoga-viyogarh yoga sarhjfitam

sa niscayena -yoktavyo yogo nirvinna-cetasa

When the mind is restrained and peaceful by the practice

of yoga, it becomes detached from material desires. Thus

one can perceive the self and attain happiness. Being sit-

uated in this plane of eternal bliss, which is beyond the

scope of the mundane senses and obtained through intel-

ligence, one never deviates from reality. Upon gaining this

position, one considers that there is nothing superior to

this and does not become disturbed even in the midst of

the greatest calamities. You should know that this state of

being, wherein all miseries are destroyed, is known as yoga.
