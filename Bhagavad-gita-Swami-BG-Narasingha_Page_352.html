Both ‘God’ and His non-existence are but misconcep-

tions in the minds of the so-called theist and the atheist.

However, the student of Bhagavad-gita knows that the sub-

ject matter is not a thesis about ‘God’. Bhagavad-gita is a

discourse aimed at enlightenment in the Absolate Trath.

The Absolute Truth contains all things known, knowable

and unknown — before the universe, within the universe,

after the universe and beyond the universe.

VERSE 20

प्रकृतिं पुरुषं चैव विच्यनादी उभावपि ।

विकारांश्च गुणांश्चैव विदि प्रकृतिसंभवान्‌ ॥२०॥

धी purusarh caiva viddbyanadt ubbav api

vikararns ca gunans caiva viddbi prakrti-sambbavan

You should know that both material nature and the living

beings are without beginning. Try to understand that all

transformations and the modes of nature originate from

material nature.

VERSE 21

कार्यकारणकतुतवे हेतुः प्रकृतिरुच्यते |

पुरुषः सुखदुःखानां भोक्तृत्वे हेतुरुच्यते URE

karya-karana-kartrtve betub prakrtir ucyate

purusab sukba-dubkhanarh bboktrtve hetur ucyate

It is said that material nature is the source of all causes and

effects. The living beings are said to be the cause of their

own happiness and distress.
