To be transcendental to material nature means to

accept the Vedas and thus be situated beyond the modes

of nature in the realm of visuddha-sattva, pure goodness.

When one’s faith is situated in pure goodness, one wot-

ships the Supreme Person, Krsna. This is the highest stage

of monotheism — the acceptance of one Supreme Being.

Pute-goodness is described by Siva as follows:

sattvarh visuddham vasudeva-Sabditarh

yad tyate tatra puman apavrtab

One should always worship Krsna in pure goodness.

Pure goodness is always pure consciousness in which

the Absolute Truth, known as Vasudeva, is revealed

without any covering. (Srimad Bhagavatam 4.3.23)
