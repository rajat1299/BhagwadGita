Karma~yoga (bhakti-yoga) on the other hand, is easily

adoptable by anyone at any stage and leads quickly to the

ultimate goal of life. In karma~yoga one performs devo-

tional activities and follows basic principles of purity such

as no intoxication, no illicit sex, no gambling and no eating

of meat, fish ot eggs. These principles can be easi Ly adopted

by anyone. Similarly, the devotional activity of chanting

the maba-mantra (hare krsna hare krsna krsna krsna bare

bare, bare rama bare rama rama rama bare bare) 15 also

easily taken up along with meditation on the form of Sti

Krsna. Mantra meditation is the recommended process of

worshipping Krsna in Kali-yuga. Therefore karma—yoga 15

given first preference in Bhagavad-gita.

However, when sannyisa~yoga 1s dovetailed with kar-

ma-yoga and the sannyast performs any and all sacrifices,

austerities and shares the knowledge of Bhagavad-gita with

others, according to the desires of Krsna, then such a san-

nyast becomes most perfect and is naturally accepted as the

gara or teacher of both paths.
