Anuvttti

The sacred conversation between Sti Kysna and Arjuna

ends with verse seventy-three wherein Arjuna says that his

delusion and doubts have all been dispelled. Thus Arjuna

agrees to follow Krsna’s instructions. This is the ideal

relationship between gura and disciple. The guru must

dispel the delusion and doubts of a disciple by delivering

the message of Sti Krsna found in Bhagavad-gita, without

change ot adulteration, and the disciple must be willing to

follow such instructions.

The potency of hearing Krsna’s message is also con-

firmed in Srimad Bhagavatam as follows:

Sravatarn sva-kathab krsnab punya-Sravana-kirtanab

brdy-antab stho by-abbadrani vidbunoti subrt satan

Sti Kysna is the friend of the pious. He removes all

inauspiciousness from the heart of those who have

developed the desire to hear His message, which is

vittuous when properly heard and chanted. (Srimad

Bhagavatam 1.2.17)

Satijaya has been nattating the conversation between

Sti Krsna and Arjuna to Emperor Dhrtarastra and now he

expresses his own satisfaction and ecstasy. He says that

by remembering the words of Sri Krsna and by seeing His

beautiful form, he feels great ecstasy (brsya) and great

wonder (vistnaya). Satijaya then concludes with the bene-

diction that wherever thete is Sti Krsna, the Master of yoga,

and wherever there is Arjona, the sincere disciple, there will

always be prosperity, victory, opulence and righteousness.
