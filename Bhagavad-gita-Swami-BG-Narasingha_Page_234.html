Yoga and meditation are described herein. However,

they are not impersonal or self-centred processes. Medita-

tion is to be performed on the Absolute Truth, Sti Krsna —

not on abstract ideas that we ourselves are the sum total of

reality, that we are all-knowing ot that we are the supreme

controller etc. Such processes are not meditation, but

self-deception and never lead to liberation or eternal bliss.

In verse 10, Sti Krsna says that in yogic meditations

(astanga and kundalini-yoga) one should draw one’s life-

aits to the area of the eyebrows. This indicates the location

of the ajfia-cakra. There are seven cakras located in the

body that are the primaty seats of human consciousness.

Consciousness petvades the entire body, but is said to

be centred in one of the seven cakras — miiladbara-cakra

(located at the base of the genital), svadbisthana-cakra

(located at the base of the spinal cord), manipiira-cakra

(located in the navel area), anabata-cakra (located in the

heart), visuddba-cakra (located in the throat), ajfia-cakra

(located in between the eyebrows) and the sabasrara-cakra

ocated at the top of the head).
