ग BHAGAVAD-GITA oye

dambbo darpo’bbimanas ca krodhab parusyameva ca

ajfianarh cabbijatasya partha sampadam asurtm

Pride, arrogance, conceit, anger, cruelty and ignorance

— these are the qualities manifest in one born of an impi-

ous nature (asura).

VERSE 5

देवी संपद्विमोक्षाय निबन्धायासुरी मता |

मा शुचः संपदं दैवीमभिजातोऽसि पाण्डव ॥५॥

daivi sampad virnoksaya nibandhayasurt mata

ma Sucab sampadarh daivim abbijato’st pandava

The qualities of a deva lead one to liberation, while the

qualities of an asura cause bondage. Fear not, O Pandava,

for you are born of the nature of a deva.

VERSE 6

at भूतसर्गो लोकेऽस्मिन्देव आसुर एव च ।

देवो विस्तरशः प्रोक्त आसुरं पाथं मे श्रणु ॥६॥

dvau bhiuta-sargau loke’smin daiva 5 eva ca

daivo vistarasab prokta asurarh partha me Srau

Partha, there are two types of people born into this world

—the deva and the asura. I have described devas in detail.

Now listen to Me as I describe the asuras.
