For one who has subdued the mind, the mind is a friend.

However, for one who has not controlled the mind, the

mind is the greatest enemy.

VERSE 7

जितात्मनः प्रशान्तस्य परमात्मा समाहितः ।

खीतोष्णसुख दुःखेषु तथा मानापमानयोः ॥७॥

jitatmanab prasantasya paramatma samabitah

Sitosna-sukba-dubkhesu tatha manapamanayob

Those who have subdued the mind and are calm, attain

realisation of Paramatma (Super Consciousness). For such

persons heat and cold, happiness and distress and honour

and dishonour are all the same.

Many commentators on Bhagavad-gita have mentioned

that the method of meditation spoken of in the Sixth

Chapter is derived from the eightfold process of yoga

known as astafiga~yoga. The famous compiler of the Yoga-

siitras, Patanjali has explained the sequential order of the

astanga~yoga system as follows:

First, one should practice yama that consists of obser-

vances such as rising from bed before suntise, taking bath,

study of the Vedas and performing paja (rituals).

Ntyama consists of contro, ling the senses by following

regulative ptinciples such as no intoxication, no illicit sex,

no gambling and no eating of meat, fish or eggs.

Next one begins the practice of asana by physically

conditioning the body through systematic bodily exercises

and postures aimed at toning and bringing into balance

one’s entire physical organism.
