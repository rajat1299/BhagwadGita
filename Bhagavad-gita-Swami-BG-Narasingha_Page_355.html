They ate without a first cause because they are Saktis, or

potencies of the Absolute Truth who is Himself without

origin. In other words, the cause of all causes is Sti Krsna.

Thus, He is known as sarva~karana-karanam.

Though material nature and the living beings are eter-

nal and without beginning, they nonetheless have distinctly

different qualities and characteristics. They are not the

same. Material nature is described as the body, senses and

other elements as well as the transformations of qualities

such as happiness, distress, lamentation and illusion. The

living beings are patt and parcel of the Supreme Person.

They ate sac-cid-ananda — having the constitution of eter-

nity, knowledge and bliss. When the iving eings identify

the self as the material body, they saffer ander the miser-

ies of happiness and distress and perpetually transmigrate

from one body to the next in the cycle of birth and death.

One of the greatest questions in life, namely, “Where

do we come from?” is certainly answered in Bhagavad-gita.

Yet the use of the word anadi in verse 20 has encouraged

some thinkers to conclude that the living beings have

always been in the material world. In other words, that

although the living beings ate eternal, they begin in the

material universe and have always been in the material

universe. This conclusion however is not supported by

ptevious aciryas of the Bhagavad-gita such as Vigvanatha

Caktavarti, Baladeva Vidyabhiisana and others. To them,

anadi means beginningless, before time began.
