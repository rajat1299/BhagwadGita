There ate sufficient facilities to maintain everyone on this

facilities are simply mismanaged. And more to blame than

world to easily solve the problems facing humanity, par-

ticularly that of hunger — but that wealth is being hoarded

by a very small minority of people. They have amassed

so much that no individual could spend or use that much

wealth within one or even a dozen lifetimes. Corporate

giants receive millions of dollars in annual bonuses while

millions of children die each year due to malnutrition. Is

this not shameful?

Charity, like other things discussed in this chapter, is

also in the different modes of nature according to how it is

given and whom it is given to. There 13 charity in goodness,

passion and ignorance as stated by Sti Krsna above, but

ultimately the highest charity is to give that which helps

the haman being bring an end to all material miseries and

even to death itself. Such charity is the distribution of the

spititual wealth found in Bhagavad-gita.

The root cause of all suffering in the world is a lack of

understanding of who we are, where we have come from,

what the purpose of life is and where we will go at the

time of death. One who understands these things from the

perspective of Bhagavad-gita becomes fall in knowledge,

free from the illusion of the body as the self and ultimately

defeats death. This is the highest gift and greatest charity

that one can give a fellow human being.
