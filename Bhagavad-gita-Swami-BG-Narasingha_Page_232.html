However, it is our experience of the world that death

is often accompanied by great pain, confusion and bewil-

derment of memory. Therefore, it would seem that to

remember Krsna at the time of death is no easy task. Death

may come swiftly at any time, without a moments notice

or even during sleep, thus impeding one’s remembrance of

Krsna. In this regard Kalagekhara Alvar writes in Mukun-

da-mala Stotram as follows:

krsna tvadiya-pada-pankaja-pafijarantam

adyaiva me visatu manasa-raja-barhsab

prana-prayana-samaye kapha-vata-pittaih

kanthavarodbana-vidhau smaranari kutas te

0 Krsna, please help me die quickly so the swan

of my mind may become encircled by the stem of

Your lotus feet. Otherwise at the time of my final

reath, when my throat is choked up, how will it be

possible for me to think of You? (Mukunda-mala

Stotram 33)

For the bhakti-yogi Krsna takes all his many endeavours,

practices and services into consideration. Even if the bhak-

ti-yogi is unable to remember Krsna at the time of death,

Kysna will certainly remember him. Sri Krsna is never for-

getful under any circumstance and thus He swiftly delivers

His devotee from the clutches of death. Krsna Himself

confirms this in the Varaba Purana as follows:

yadi vitadi-dosena mad bhakto math ca na smaret

abarh smarami mad bhaktab nayami paramarh gatim
